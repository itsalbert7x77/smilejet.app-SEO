The Treatment Process: What to Expect

Understanding the dental implant timeline helps you plan your trip effectively. Here's the typical process when getting implants in Vietnam:

1

Before You Arrive: Free Virtual Consultation

Submit your X-rays or dental photos through SmileJet. Receive personalized treatment plans and quotes from multiple clinics within 24 hours. Compare options and choose your preferred clinic before booking flights.

2

Trip 1 (Days 1–5): Consultation & Implant Placement

Day 1: In-person consultation, 3D CBCT scan, finalized treatment plan. Day 2: Implant surgery (typically 1–2 hours per implant). Days 3–5: Recovery, follow-up checks, and enjoy Vietnam. If bone grafting is needed, this is done during the same visit.

3

Healing Period (3–6 Months at Home)

The implant integrates with your jawbone (osseointegration). Your clinic provides remote follow-up via WhatsApp or email. You can eat and function normally with temporary teeth if needed.

4

Trip 2 (Days 1–3): Crown Fitting

Return to Vietnam for the final step. Your permanent crown is custom-fabricated and fitted. Final adjustments ensure a perfect bite and natural appearance. You leave with your complete new smile.

Same-Day Options: Some clinics offer immediate-loading implant protocols where temporary crowns are placed on the same day as surgery. Ask about this when requesting your quote on SmileJet.

Planning Your Dental Trip to Vietnam

Flights

Major international airports in Ho Chi Minh City (SGN), Hanoi (HAN), and Da Nang (DAD). Direct flights available from most Asian capitals, Australia, and select European cities. Budget airlines like VietJet and AirAsia offer affordable fares.

Accommodation

Vietnam offers excellent value accommodation. Quality hotels near dental clinics range from $30–$80/night. Many clinics can recommend nearby hotels popular with dental tourists.

Visa

Most nationalities can obtain an e-visa online (valid 90 days) or visa on arrival. Citizens of many countries enjoy visa-free entry for 15–45 days. Check current requirements for your nationality before travel.

Insurance

Standard travel insurance covers general medical emergencies. Some providers offer dental tourism-specific policies. Ask your SmileJet coordinator about warranty coverage and what happens if you need follow-up treatment at home.
